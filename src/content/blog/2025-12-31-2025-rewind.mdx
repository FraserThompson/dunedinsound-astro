As far as years go I reckon this one was pretty good for music in Dunedin. There were heaps of good gigs as per usual, and a bit of media attention on the scene towards the end as the cherry on top.

## Trajectory Analysis

<Image size="blog" responsiveImage={frontmatter.responsiveImages['gig-velocity-over-time-25']} alt="Gig velocity over time (a graph showing number of gigs attended slumping during 2021 and 2022, then rising until the current year)"/>

Suffice to say this year was massive, the biggest on record. In my [2024 retrospective](/blog/2024_retrospective_10_years_of_dunedinsoundcom/) I wrote that I was "unlikely to return to peak levels due to the unfortunate fact that I'm not 20 anymore." Well 2024 self, turns out I am 20. Or maybe there's another explanation.

First of all I had a helper who documented a handful of gigs I was unable to attend. This is how I managed to get [two](/gigs/high-altar-adoration-of-the-mystic-lamb-tour/) [simultaneous](/gigs/deathro-aotearoa-tour/) gigs up on 7/3/25 for example. Unfortunately I still haven't found a way to be two places at once but this is the next best thing. 

Secondly, of course this was a Lines of Flight year, which means three gigs (and [the artist talk](/gigs/lines-of-flight-2025-friday-day/), thanks to the aforementioned helper).

And lastly there was the historic, the momentous, FIVE GIG WEEKEND which was basically just me pushing myself to see if I still had it in me and I guess I did, although I was basically dead by the end and I don't really want to do that again. I am definitely not 20.

<Image size="blog" responsiveImage={frontmatter.responsiveImages['fivegigweekend']} alt="Screenshot of an Instagram story featuring five gigs in one weekend and a poll asking if it's possible (67% yes)" caption="67"/>

- [Friday: Stapled noise handbook fundraiser](/gigs/stapled-noise-handbook-fundraiser/)

## Memorable gigs (chronological order)

The start of the year feels like infinity ago. As usual this is not an exhaustive list of every gig I enjoyed, just a handful which stuck with me. Check the [gig catalogue](/gigs/) if you want to scroll through everything that happened.

### [C.O.F.F.I.N Aotearoa Tour 25 - February 8 ](/gigs/coffin-aotearoa-tour-25)

<Image size="blog" responsiveImage={frontmatter.responsiveImages['coffin']} alt="C.O.F.F.I.N Aotearoa Tour 25"/>

Children of Finland Fighting in Norway are an Australian hard rock and roll band who played the Crown Hotel as part of their tour in February. Couldn't have picked a better venue for this sort of band and there was a high concentration of sweaty shirtless men (it was hot as hell, the drippy heatpump had no hope of keeping up). As things progressed the crowd morphed into a moist wriggling amorphous blob of hair and skin…. Pretty sure this was the gig which put that crack in the door glass.

They came back in November for a second 2025 Aotearoa tour, this one skipped Dunedin but I don't hold that against them and I'm sure they'll be back.

### [DEATHRO - March 7](/gigs/deathro-aotearoa-tour) + [GUITAR WOLF - March 9](/gigs/guitar-wolf-nz-tour-2025)

<Image size="blog" responsiveImage={frontmatter.responsiveImages['deathro']} alt="DEATHRO Aotearoa Tour 25"/>
<Image size="blog" responsiveImage={frontmatter.responsiveImages['guitar-wolf']} alt="Guitar Wolf NZ Tour 25"/>

I'm grouping these together because they happened the same weekend and they both came from Japan which I think is a fun coincidence.

DEATHRO plays a specifically Japanese form of punk rock from the 80s called BEATROCK which seemed to be like J-rock but faster and with lots of jumping around. It was very cool to see, especially with Displeasure as his backing band (they toured Japan together, real cool stuff). Love this sort of cross-pollination, when smaller artists from overseas come and play small venues with local bands it's always a treat (in a similar vein, Dale Kerrigan toured China this year!!).

I actually didn't intend on going to Guitar Wolf, but someone (Simon) put me on the door at the last minute (like a few hours before) so I couldn't say no… And I'm so grateful because it was amazing. Guitar Wolf have come to Dunedin many times in the past, which meant a very multi-generational crowd, children with their parents and so on. And the sort of fun energy Guitar Wolf put into their live show appeals to everyone, despite the dangerous edge (they wear leather jackets!!! + the bassist kept swearing at the crowd in Japanese…) Pretty wild stuff, love this sort of rip roaring unapologetic rock and roll.

Also, Guitar Wolf wanted, no, needed a stage so they could stage dive, so they rented one, which was such a great idea that now Pearl Diver has a permanent proper stage, which sure beats the band in the corner type situation. Very cool to see this venue stepping into its potency.

### [Ensnared - April 19](/gigs/ensnared)

<Image size="blog" responsiveImage={frontmatter.responsiveImages['ens']} alt="Ensnared gig"/>

This was an art show which culminated in a gig at the back of a vacant shop on George Street, made possible with the help of [Dunedin Dream Brokerage](/gigs/series/dunedin_dream_brokerage/).

I find it very enriching to experience new and unusual spaces, and this weird little spot on George Street was one of the coolest I've been to in a hot while. The front was your typical soulless commercial space, but down the back there was an almost attic-like (as in, [The Attic](/venues/the_attic)) room complete with mezzanine balcony. It was perfect for this sort of gig. And the bathrooms... Oh my god the bathrooms.

<Image size="blog" responsiveImage={frontmatter.responsiveImages['bathroom']} alt="Strange empty corner in bathrooms"/>

Hope to see more things here. Also helped that the lineup was impeccable, the highlight being [IVY](/artists/ivy) as usual.

Incredible spaces are tucked away everywhere, and the only thing between you and them is a bit of bureaucracy which is honestly probably not that bad since it's cool people who want to help you make it work. Someone do something with that giant empty office building opposite Woof.

### [We forgot - April 25](/gigs/we-forgot)

<Image size="blog" responsiveImage={frontmatter.responsiveImages['forgot']} alt="Synthesizers on the table"/>

This makes the list for a couple of reasons. For one it was definitely the least attended gig of 2025, pretty much exclusively the performers and our friends. Secondly, I played (conflict of interest disclosure). I thought the concept was great, just a table in the middle of the room with lots of sound making devices and then we just ducked in and out as we wanted (we set up some TVs with game consoles too) and it was a fun way to spend ANZAC day. Shoutout Jos for putting it on.

### [FlipperFest gigs - July and August](/gigs/series/flipperfest)

<Image size="blog" responsiveImage={frontmatter.responsiveImages['flipperfest']} alt="Flip-Pint Night at Home"/>

FlipperFest is a cool wee series of gigs to raise money for the yellow eyed penguins (so they can buy nice things for themselves) and this year had old man dunedinsound dot com venturing into the heart of studentville for a house gig. Who invited their dad? Apart from getting to feel really old, I also got to see DJ Bax destroy an underpowered PA with his brutal, relentless, unapologetic, donking. And later on I got a taste of some of the newer student bands I tend to miss at the Dropkicks and Crown Hotel variants, and my main takeaway was the kids love nu metal. Maybe things are looking up.

### [Lines of Flight - October 16 to 18](/gigs/series/lines_of_flight)

<Image size="blog" responsiveImage={frontmatter.responsiveImages['linesofflight']} alt="Lines of Flight"/>

As expected, Lines of Flight was a truly transformative experience. Among a lot of factors I think it's the variation and attention paid to sequencing the lineup which does it for me. Like yeah, cool, give me the improvised string section (Mystery String Quartet), but let me listen to a maximalist Britney Spears cover too (Blue Cheese). It's just great. There's too much to even talk about here, truly a mammoth effort and invaluable addition to Ōtepoti's cultural repertóire.

### [Brainwave Ill Intent Release Tour - November 16](/gigs/brainwave-ill-intent-release-tour/)

<Image size="blog" responsiveImage={frontmatter.responsiveImages['brainwave']} alt="Brainwave Ill Intent Release Tour"/>

The sun was shining, the vibes were good, and Wellington hardcore band Brainwave were in Dunedin in the midst of their Aotearoa tour for a Sunday afternoon show at YOURS. Bit of an unusual decision to do a Sunday afternoon hardcore show (I assume it was by necessity) and it was quite under attended as a result, but I honestly loved it. Relentless riffs and positive energy is a great way to cap off a weekend. Often with small crowds you really feel the solidarity, everyone's there with a shared understanding and purpose.

### [Koizilla the final jam - November 28](/gigs/koizilla---the-final-jam/)

<Image size="blog" responsiveImage={frontmatter.responsiveImages['koizilla']} alt="Koizilla - the final jam"/>

After taking the dunedinsound.com "most gigs" crown from The Rothmans in 2024, Koizilla was finally able to call it quits. In all seriousness though Koizilla were an extremely important band to me and many others in the scene, through their support for other bands and tireless work ethic, not to mention some absolute bangers. Their final gig felt like a coming together for the community. The respect in the crowd was palpable; it's not often that a band can do an acoustic number for their final song to a dead silent crowd at the Crown Hotel. 

And this cultural legacy is now [cemented in Audioculture](https://www.audioculture.co.nz/profile/koizilla). HUUUUUGE aroha.

### [Harsh Noise Soft Coffees - Throughout the year](/gigs/series/harsh_noise_soft_coffee/)

<Image size="blog" responsiveImage={frontmatter.responsiveImages['hnsc']} alt="Harsh Noise Soft Coffee 2 Poster"/>

Conflict of interest disclosure: I help organise these. They are my baby.

Kind of a giant year for HNSC, our bi-monthly series of daytime all-ages koha experimental gigs. After a singular gig in 2024, we put on six further iterations in 2025. Honestly it was very cool to see so many new noise/experimental/other musicians in such a relaxed space. Thanks to everyone who played and turned up, there will be more.

## Nerd corner

This was the first full year of dunedinsound.com on Astro after the [great migration from Gatsby](/blog/how_i_rewrote_my_huge_gatsby_site_in_astro_and_learned_to_love_it_in_the_process/) and I've continued to tweak and refine things.

<Image size="blog" responsiveImage={frontmatter.responsiveImages['commits']} alt="Graph of commits for dunedinsound GitHub repository"/>

### Change in hosting provider

Issues with MP3 playback prompted me to look at other options for hosting. It turned out S3 was sending the wrong headers for partial requests, so I moved to Cloudflare workers with R2 for the object storage. The transition was painless (the AWS API is literally compatible with R2) and it has numerous benefits: I have full control over headers now, and deploys are substantially faster (like, before it took tens of minutes, now it takes tens of seconds). Oh and my monthly hosting bill is basically nothing (like $2 as opposed to $10) because it turns out AWS is very expensive. Let me know if anyone wants a full article on this.

### Too many elements makes DOM sad

It became obvious that the artists page was untenable and kind of annoying; an image for each artist doesn't really communicate anything useful, and when there are like 800 artists that's a hell of a lot of bits and bytes.

So to solve this I did two things: redesign the artists page to just be a list of artist names, and implement a virtualized list component which I also used on the gigs page. This means even if there's hundreds of elements, the only elements in the DOM will be the ones visible plus a few more, then as the user scrolls it'll delete invisible ones and add visible ones. Usually this would be overkill but when you've got that many things it makes a huge difference to scrolling performance.

### The eternal quest for peak optimization continues

I decided to look at my file lookups and see if there was anything I could do to speed that up without getting silly. What me and VS Code Copilot landed on (nothing wrong with vibe coding as long as its a tool not a crutch and you understand what it's doing, a dialogue rather than a 'do this for me') was local sqlite databases which indexed what files I have and where they are so instead of filesystem lookups (slow when it's hundreds of thousands of files) it can just load that into memory then query it. Neat. Again, let me know if anyone wants a full article on this.

### Series

I noticed there were quite a few gigs which belonged together but this wasn't represented on the website, so I made 'series' pages which I'm hoping are helpful for answering questions like "ok but what IS Lines of Flight". This is a work in progress.

### Mini XLR termination

The connection between my ear microphones and battery box was always a weak link (it was a 3.5mm jack and sometimes got crackly) so now this terminates to a mini XLR connector I got from Jaycar and doesn't get crackly ever. Ever.

### Minor design changes

The menu links are rounded now so they look all nice and tabby. I think I changed the colour scheme (I go through phases where I decide it sucks and change it). And I tidied up the title sections on content pages to be more readable. Also, articles like this one are now in light mode, and I tweaked the text spacing and font size for readability. You've been reading it so I guess it worked.

### Storage problem and solution

Guess which dumb idiot built a highly redundant NAS in 2024 and then immediately ran out of space.

So as the year went on, my internal drives steadily filled up with gig media with no home, and I had to rethink the whole strategy. Data loss would be catastrophic so there needed to be a backup obviously, but redundant local storage is an expensive way to do it. Backblaze has unlimited per system backup for a minimal annual fee, which I was already using for my internal drives, but the catch is it only works for drives which are directly connected to the system, so not network drives ie the NAS I built.

I came up with a plan: Buy a giant external drive and just back that up into Backblaze as part of my existing unlimited plan. Sure having everything on one drive is risky compared to a redundant array, but the data is only at risk before it's backed up into Backblaze which should happen pretty soon after ingest. If the drive fails I can restore it from Backblaze pretty quickly thanks to gigabit internet #gigatown.

So just last week I took advantage of boxing day sales to get a big ole 16TB external for a pretty oof $600 or so, which should hopefully do me for another 5 years or so, and after that its future me's problem. Good luck.
