This year (well, last year) I went to the council run New Years Eve celebration in the Octagon, a first for me because the previous incarnations have never been enticing enough to overcome the desire to just stay home and watch YouTube then go to bed. As is tradition.

But somehow this year, against all odds, the council had wrangled a band lineup which seemed somewhat interesting. A couple of high school bands, a student band, all of whom I'd seen before and was not opposed to seeing again. So after a brief Woof session I ambled up to catch the bands.

<Image size="blog" responsiveImage={frontmatter.responsiveImages['1']} alt="Looking down on the octagon with a decent sized crowd early on"/>

The first band was Deaf Racoon, who delivered some sort of… I don’t know what you’d call it, elevated pop punk? Like there’d be a skater punk sort of chorus, then it’d turn into Radiohead-esque wiggly bassline stuff or something. It was a bit rough (non-derogatory), and I’ll take rough over a career Rock band playing "Rock Music” any day.

<Image size="blog" responsiveImage={frontmatter.responsiveImages['2']} alt="Deaf Racoon playing"/>

<Image size="blog" responsiveImage={frontmatter.responsiveImages['2-1']} alt="The crowd watching Deaf Racoon"/>

By the way, the two high school bands had both secured finalist at Smokefreerockquest earlier in the year, apparently a first for Otago (having two bands in the final) and probably what contributed to them being picked to play.

Between bands there was a DJ who managed to mix party bangers with just enough finesse, but not too much, to keep things fun. Like whatever, anyone can crossfade a playlist, but this guy was doing actual mixing, dropping airhorns left right and centre... Some of the mixes I was like damn, really? “Don't you worry child” into “We like to party” by vengaboys? With a long crossfade? But I admired the tenacity. It made me smile. And it even got pockets of the demure crowd grooving and singing along.

Then Seek Help! played. A stark contrast to the mediocre drunken dad rock of Octagon NYE past, it was scary young feminist band with harsh vocals. And guess what, people didn't flee, or at least not enough people to matter (I'm sure there were some who hated it and took refuge in Vault 21 or whatever). But the crowd was mostly either actively enjoying or at least appreciating. 

<Image size="blog" responsiveImage={frontmatter.responsiveImages['3']} alt="Seek Help playing"/>

And this crowd was about as representative as it can be right? Cruise ship tourists excluded. Honestly most gigs I go to are pretty exclusive, like not literally excluding anyone, but just inherently because of what they are, they're not for everyone. In my opinion that's completely fine because not everything has to be for everyone (and in fact, maybe it's better if not everything is). But seeing a crowd of "everyone" bopping along to riot girl punk was inspiring, I'm not sure how this lineup came to be but I praise whatever council staff made it happen. These sorts of risks have wide cultural impacts.

<Image size="blog" responsiveImage={frontmatter.responsiveImages['4']} alt="The crowd watching Seek Help"/>

And then IVY, who sounded like a billion bucks, absolutely world class and the audience clearly felt that. They were transfixed. Jesse’s soaring vocals reverberated around the natural amphitheatre of the Octagon (the sound was very good directly in front of the line arrays, although to me the mixes were consistently a bit too light on guitar) and as the sun set the lighting rig really worked its magic. Can't wait to see what else these guys can do, they've got an incredible thing going. I'd love to see them experimenting with sounds more outside their comfort zone, but there's plenty of time for that.

<Image size="blog" responsiveImage={frontmatter.responsiveImages['5-2']} alt="IVY playing"/>

<Image size="blog" responsiveImage={frontmatter.responsiveImages['5-3']} alt="IVY playing"/>

This was not Rhythm and Alps. There were families present, even late into the night. There were food trucks, tables set amongst the crowd, but no alcohol outside of the octagon bars, which weren't as full as I expected (Woof on the other hand... At capacity with a massive line outside. No hope of getting back in once I'd left). The vibes were positive but not exuberant, and honestly that's exactly what I wanted. Despite the MCs admirable attempts to practice an enthusiastic <strong>"happy new year!!!"</strong>, it seemed like we were more comfortable with a <small>"happy new year"</small>. And I think that's fine. Many ways to crumble an omelette.

<Image size="blog" responsiveImage={frontmatter.responsiveImages['6']} alt="The crowd watching IVY"/>

It was just nice, I'm not sure why I expected a messy pissup (I guess throughout my life this is what most NYE celebrations end up being? And also what most of my late night octagon experiences end up being...) but it was refreshing to have a public event where everyone is just chilling. Just people peopling without toxic bad decision juice flowing through their veins making them do dumb shit.

<Image size="blog" responsiveImage={frontmatter.responsiveImages['7']} alt="Food trucks"/>

I didn’t stick around for the countdown. I went home after IVY at around 11pm, a decision I feel entirely comfortable with. I didn't drink, went to bed early, and woke up in 2026 fresh as a daisy. Not at all what I expected from enduring an Octagon NYE. The covers band after IVY seemed fine and a solid choice for the home stretch, but not something I was personally interested in. Which is fine because, as I mentioned earlier, not everything has to be for everyone. In 2026 we should all strive to experience something which isn't for us, be it a covers band or harsh noise. 

I haven't checked the ODT comments yet to gauge the general reaction, but no doubt there will be the usual loud complainers. I think it's easy to let these voices dominate our worldviews, but that doesn't reflect reality. I think more people than we think are actually open to new and interesting experiences, or ones which might be challenging. And I think NYE 25 proves that.
